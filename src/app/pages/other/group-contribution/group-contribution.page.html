A group of Fontys students has been tasked with designing a solution for Additive Industriesâ€™ wish to allow users to monitor their 3D printers remotely. Additive Industries builds industrial 3D printers, of which the most popular option is to print metal parts.

Additive Industries has expressed its wishes for a solution to be designed that would allow operators of the printer to be able to do more tasks remotely, such as monitoring one or multiple printers, or queue new print jobs, all while taking into account that a company might own multiple printers.

Execution

Design Challenge

To make it clear for everyone what we were desgining, for who etc. We created a Design Challenge and updated it accordingly any time we saw fit to do so. The history and iterations of the Design Challenge can be found here.

Project Plan

At the start of the project, we created a project plan to give us and Additive Industries a clear overview of what we were planning on doing. As a group, we made a project plan to do so. I created parts of the research for the project plan which can be found here inside the document.

The creation of the project plan was not very special, it was simply a chore in that way. Yet we did not slack our way to complete the document.

2D Print House Interview

As described within it's own little place here, I did an interview with my dad about the current way of working at the print house he works at. This reseach was solely done by me to gather insights which could be used for the ideation phase of the human centered design design thinking.

Stakeholder Profiles

As we came closer to the ideation phase, we still needed users to design for and as we did not have them clear, we did not really know who we were desiging for. As such, Aleksandar and me created stakeholder profiles to make it clearer to us and Additive Industries as well, who we were designing for and why.

The creation of the document and the profiles took longer than expected, yet me and Aleksandar created very detailed and thought out persona's which can be relatable.

How Might We questions

When we got all the insights we wanted from the research performed, we started to create How Might We questions. With these questions, we answered them through concepts and others. We wrote these down inside the first ideas document to showcase the line of thought which went into it.

The creation of the How Might We questions was done through the IDEO ideation phase. We started with all the research from each individual and shared it with the group.

Breakroom testing

To validate our prototype with the Additive Industries employees, Bart and I created a breakroom test to do at Additive Industries. We created a test plan, a survey and a test report to document the full research. The responses were slim at first, but kicked up towards the end of the test. This gave us insights to later improve the prototype once more and create good recommendations for the continuation of the project.

Design system

Towards the end of the project, I started to create a Design System for the group. I set up a GitHub repository to work out of and created a in-depth design system inside of the storybooks with Aleksandar and Max K. The final design system can be found here.

The creation of this design system was fun, yet very much work to do. The collaboration with Aleksandar on this was interesting as I helped him a ton with learning this way of documenting the design system.

Transferability document

As our final design of the prototype came to a finish, we became ready to transfer the project. Bart and me made this document for the ICT in practice symposium and this would later be the base for the transferability document that woul go towards Additive Industries.

Presentations

During the length of the project, I gave a few presentations to our client during demo's and our bi-weekly progress meetings with them. This made me better at presenting in a way, as I never wanted to do so up until now. I found it to be quite fun to make and present our project to Additive Industries as it also made clear what we had done to the group aswell.

Reflection

This project has been one of the more challenging projects I've done throughout my study. As there were others involved with different ways of thinking and techniques, there was a lot of friction between me and the group. This became hard at times as in the end, I felt like I and other group members were doing more work then others.

In addition to this, it was hard that Jurgen would not respond as quick as possible as that caused for delays within the project timeline. This is one of the frustations which was spoken out multiple times resulting in better communications down the road.

My role as group leader was sometimes vague for me, I thought I was expected to do one thing while others expected me to do another thing. During the project, it felt like the group members wanted me to always fit into the role they expected me to be while forgetting that I was also learning to become a group leader and better myself in that role as well. This was frustating at times, yet we made it work in the end after communicating about it a ton of times.

In the end, we came together as a team and did some amazing work for Additive Industries which I am proud of. As well that I think I've developed as a professional yet again in, for example, the role as group leader and international group member.
