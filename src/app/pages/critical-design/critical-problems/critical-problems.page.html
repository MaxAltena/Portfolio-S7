Assignment

Make a list of plus-minus 10 small or big problems. These can be about all kinds of topics: personal, global, economical, political, cultural etc. Try to formulate them very specifically.

Execution

I wrote down some general problems that I was having and some very detailed ones. I requested feedback on this document where Woody gave the feedback that these problems were diverse and sometimes very detailed. This was good, as it gave a good insight of my problems and in the end values as well.

Reflection

The problems that I came up with also reflect on the values that I have. I later noticed this when working on the next assignment, the personal manifesto. Doing these assignments might be hard and not fun at the start, but in the end give you insights or views that you wouldn't normally have.

Learning outcomes

While writing down these problems, I am starting to find problems rather than solving them. Behind these problems are questions that I am asking myself, why is it this way? This is one of the first steps of using and applying Critical Design as Dunne & Raby defined.
